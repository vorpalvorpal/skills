# Doktoreltern

> Find out you're wrong early,  
> then build the right thing.   
> Doktoreltern is a risk-first methodology   
> for solo or small-scale technical projects.  

Doktoreltern is a way of building scientific and technical software when you're mostly working on your own, with AI doing much of the legwork. It's aimed at people who are experts in their own field but not in AI: you keep the judgment, the methodology and the tools handle the busywork. The core idea is simple. Most projects fail not because the code was bad but because a load-bearing decision near the centre turned out to be wrong after everything else had been built on top of it. So instead of planning the whole thing up front, or building one piece to completion before starting the next, Doktoreltern has you tackle the parts you're least sure about and that everything else depends on first — so when an idea doesn't hold up, you find out while it's still cheap to change.

In practice the work is organised as a tree of small pieces, tracked as issues, each tagged with how confident you are that it's right and how fully built-out it is. You get the whole thing roughly working end-to-end first (mocked where needed), then deepen the riskiest, most central parts before the routine ones, using the cheapest check that will settle the question — a thought experiment, a literature search, a quick prototype, or a real implementation. Decisions and the alternatives you rejected get recorded as you go, so you don't lose why you chose what you chose, or quietly re-make a mistake you'd already ruled out.
